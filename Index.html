All the main thing that happens happens for the reason. The main thing that matters the most is the thing that makes you curious.

Can CCTVs curb Police violence? On the 2nd of December 2020, a three-judge bench comprising Justices R.F Nariman, K.M. Joseph, and Aniruddha Bose ordered the installation of CCTV cameras in premises of investigative agencies that carry out interrogation and have the power to arrest an individual. Even if it is an important step to constrain custodial torture at the premises of these agencies. It can only have a serious effect if complemented with long-standing reforms to curb the practice of torture and deep-rooted culture of police violence against detenu. 3 torture. Moreover, this does not discuss other aspects of extra-judicial killings in India, for example, the extrajudicial killing of the 4 accused of the Hyrderbad rape case in 2019. Whilst order is an essential step in countering torture to track the police by CCTVs, but its efficacy relies on wider changes. It is for the Supreme court to look after the effective implementation of this order and create a robust system and concurrently fill in the loopholes of police violence. Kumar Kartikeya is a student of law based out of New Delhi
